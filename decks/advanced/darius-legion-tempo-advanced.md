## Why This Is Advanced

Every `[Legion]` card in this list is conditional on play *order*, not just
what's in hand: the bonus only fires if another card was already played
that turn. That means the pilot has to plan which card goes down first
every single turn, not just which cards to play — playing a Legion payoff
too early wastes it, and playing cheap non-Legion cards without a follow-up
plan wastes the setup. `Noxian Guillotine` is the sharpest example: it's a
delayed-kill spell on its own, but an instant kill if Legion is active —
the same card, two very different removal speeds, decided entirely by
sequencing.

## How To Play

1. Lead every turn with the cheapest card in hand (`Legion Rearguard`,
   `Daring Poro`, `Hextech Ray`, `Cleave`, `Detonate`) purely to turn on
   Legion for everything played after it.
2. Sequence `Noxian Guillotine` *after* a setup card whenever possible — an
   instant kill is worth far more than a delayed one against a unit that
   might get buffed or moved away before "the next time it takes damage."
3. Play `Darius - Trifarian` early in the turn if it's your first card, so
   the second card played triggers its own +2 Might and ready — it can
   attack immediately.
4. Use the Legend's Exhaust ability for a Reaction-speed Energy top-up once
   Legion is already active, to help afford a bigger second or third play.
5. Save `Undying Legion` for a turn you've already set up Legion and have a
   dead unit in the trash worth recurring.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- one cheap setup card: `Legion Rearguard`, `Daring Poro`, or `Hextech Ray`;
- one Legion payoff: `Trifarian Gloryseeker`, `Dangerous Duo`, or
  `Vanguard Captain`;
- `Noxian Guillotine` only with a cheap setup card already in hand to make
  it an instant kill.

Avoid keeping `Darius - Executioner` or `Scrapyard Champion` without an
early setup card — both want Legion already active to be worth their cost.

## Watch Outs

- `[Legion]` checks whether *another* card was played this turn, not
  whether it's the second card by turn order specifically — as long as one
  card came before it, order among the rest doesn't matter.
- `Noxian Guillotine`'s delayed-kill mode ("the next time it takes damage
  this turn") can miss entirely if the chosen unit never takes damage that
  turn — only rely on the delayed mode when you're confident it will fight.
- The Legend's Energy-add ability is also Legion-gated — it does nothing on
  the very first play of a turn, so don't plan around it for your opening
  play.
- This deck's early curve is intentionally full of cheap, unexciting cards
  whose whole job is enabling later payoffs — resist the urge to play a big
  Legion card first just because it's the strongest card in hand.

## Upgrade Paths

- Add `Rek'Sai - Swarm Queen` (Order) for an attack-triggered deck-dig that
  benefits from the deck's already-low curve.
- Add more cheap 1-cost spells if Legion setup ever feels inconsistent.
- For a lower-complexity version, cut `Undying Legion` and `Sun Disc` (both
  ask for tighter sequencing on top of the base Legion plan) and keep only
  the straightforward on-play Legion payoffs.
