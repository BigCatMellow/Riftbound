## Why This Is An Advanced Deck

Most decks treat [Temporary] ("Kill it at the start of its controller's
Beginning Phase, before scoring") as a cost. This deck reads it as a
free, perfectly scheduled sacrifice outlet. `LeBlanc - Deceiver` makes a
ready Reflection copy of a unit at any battlefield you conquer **or
hold**, so a stable defensive board produces one token per turn — and if
the copied unit has a [Deathknell], the token's guaranteed death cashes
it in on your schedule:

- copy `LeBlanc - Fragmented` and its Deathknell reads "If it's your
  Beginning Phase, draw 2 instead" — the Temporary kill happens exactly
  then, so every token is a delayed draw-2;
- `Karthus - Eternal` makes every one of those Deathknells trigger
  twice;
- `Wraith of Echoes` draws off the first friendly death each turn — the
  token dies every turn, on time;
- `Shard of Undoing` turns the first friendly death during your
  Beginning Phase into "each opponent must kill one of their units" —
  the token schedules that death for you every single turn;
- `Viktor - Leader` converts each dying non-Recruit token into a fresh
  Recruit body.

`LeBlanc - Everywhere at Once` is the release valve: her text shuts off
your [Temporary] triggers at her battlefield, so when a copy is worth
more alive than dead (a copied `Glasc Mixologist` holding a point, a
copied `Machine Evangel` you want as a blocker), park it with her and it
simply stays.

## Legality Notes

- Domain identity: LeBlanc - Deceiver is Mind/Order. Every main deck card
  is single-domain Mind, single-domain Order, or the Mind/Order Signature
  card `Mirror Image`.
- Copy-limit check: `LeBlanc - Fragmented` has 1 chosen-champion copy
  plus 2 main deck copies (3 total, at the limit). No other card exceeds
  3 copies.
- Signature check: `Mirror Image` (UNL-200) is LeBlanc's own signature
  (printed directly after the Legend, UNL-199); 3 copies sit exactly at
  the 3-signature limit and match the Legend's tag.
- Banned/errata check: no card used here is marked Banned in the local
  card database. `LeBlanc - Deceiver` has errata text ("Then do this:")
  clarifying the copy step; this deck uses the errata reading.
- Champion tag: `LeBlanc - Fragmented` and `LeBlanc - Everywhere at
  Once` are inferred to carry the `LeBlanc` champion tag from card name
  and the Legend's own name, per this project's convention. The source
  export has no explicit tag column — confirm the printed tag before
  tournament play.

## How To Play

1. Early game, hold a battlefield with cheap Deathknell bodies
   (`Watchful Sentry`, `Honest Broker`, `Loyal Poro`) — the Legend
   triggers on hold, so you don't need to attack to start the engine.
2. When the Legend triggers, copy the best Deathknell unit at that
   battlefield. A copied `LeBlanc - Fragmented` or `Machine Evangel` is
   usually right; the token is ready, so it can defend immediately.
3. Get `Karthus - Eternal` down before your big token turns — he doubles
   every Deathknell, including the ones on dying Reflections.
4. Sequence your Beginning Phase deliberately: the Temporary kill, `Dusk
   Rose Lab`, and `Shard of Undoing` all care about that window. With
   Shard down, the token's death forces every opponent to kill a unit
   before scoring.
5. Use `LeBlanc - Everywhere at Once` selectively — parking a copied
   `Glasc Mixologist` with her keeps a 5-Might body, but remember she
   turns off ALL your Temporary triggers at her battlefield, including
   ones you wanted (move her, or the token, if you want the death back
   on schedule).
6. `Mirror Image` is your flexible slot: copy your own engine piece at
   spell speed, or copy the opponent's best unit and swing with it for a
   turn before it evaporates.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- one cheap Deathknell body: `Watchful Sentry`, `Honest Broker`, or
  `Loyal Poro`;
- one engine multiplier: `Karthus - Eternal` or (with a body already in
  hand) `Viktor - Leader`;
- `Premonition` or `Shadow's Call` for fuel.

Avoid keeping `Shard of Undoing` or `Wraith of Echoes` in an opener with
no cheap bodies — both pay off a death engine that has to exist first.

## Watch Outs

- **Copy semantics: CONFIRMED (was flagged, now resolved).** The core
  rules' copy layer states all Traits *including the Rules Text*
  transfer, and the rulebook's own example (rule 472.1.b area) is
  literally `LeBlanc - Deceiver` playing a Reflection copy of
  `Honest Broker` — Mirror Image then copies the copy, yielding "three
  units named Honest Broker, two of which are token Copies with
  Temporary." The Reflection carries the copied unit's [Deathknell];
  the engine works as written. (Resolved against
  `Riftbound_Core_Rules_RUP3_Staging.pdf` on 2026-07-06.)
- The Legend costs a discard plus its own exhaust each activation — one
  token per turn, and the discard is real. `Premonition` and the
  Deathknell draws are in the deck partly to feed it.
- [Temporary] kills the token *before scoring*, so a Reflection holding
  a battlefield alone does NOT score that turn's hold — keep a real unit
  alongside it anywhere you need the point.
- `LeBlanc - Everywhere at Once` shuts off Temporary triggers for every
  friendly Temporary card at her battlefield, not just the one you want
  to keep — she can accidentally blank a `Shadow's Call` clock you set
  up on purpose.
- `Cull the Weak` and `Hidden Blade` still give the opponent their
  choice/draws — they're engine pieces here, not clean removal.

## Upgrade Paths

- `Dramatic Visionary` (Deathknell — Predict 2) is a fine swap for
  `Loyal Poro` if you want smoother draws over bodies.
- `Soaring Scout` and `Black Rose Dignitary` (Deathknell — channel a
  rune) turn the engine toward ramp instead of cards.
- The rulebook's own Mirror-Image-copies-the-copy example opens a
  deeper line: `Mirror Image` targeting your active Reflection
  duplicates whatever it currently copies — two scheduled Deathknells
  from one original. Test in simulation before leaning on it.
