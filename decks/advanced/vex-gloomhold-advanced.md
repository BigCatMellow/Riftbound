## Why This Is An Advanced Deck

The stun keyword usually reads as a one-turn delay. This deck chains
stun effects into hard control:

- `Vex - Apathetic` (chosen) turns her battlefield into flypaper — any
  unit played there is stunned AND can't move away that turn, so
  reinforcements arrive pre-neutralized;
- `Shadow`, Vex's signature unit, enters ready at a battlefield and
  stuns an attacker for 1 Energy + Any Rune, every turn — a repeating
  wall that also holds for the Legend's draw;
- `Leona - Zealot`'s -8 Might to stunned enemies at her battlefield
  makes the free kills literal: your 2-drops eat their 6-drops;
- `Existential Dread`'s upgrade clause (bounce if already stunned)
  converts a second stun into full tempo removal — and it has [Repeat];
- `Monch` costs 2 less and enters ready whenever anything is stunned,
  which in this deck is always;
- `Solari Shrine` pays a card every time a stunned unit dies, and
  `Eclipse Herald` readies plus grows off every stun;
- `Vex - Gloomist` herself converts the resulting quiet — you holding
  battlefields the opponent can't profitably attack — into a card per
  turn, and `Trevor Snoozebottom` mints a free Sprite off the same
  holds.

## How To Play

1. Camp battlefields early with `Shadow`, `Solari Shieldbearer`, and
   `Trevor Snoozebottom` — every quiet hold is a Legend draw and a
   Sprite.
2. Spend stuns on **attackers, during combat**: a stunned attacker deals
   nothing while your defenders hit back for full — that's removal at
   stun prices.
3. Park `Vex - Apathetic` at the battlefield they most want to
   reinforce; with her down, their fresh units arrive stunned and
   pinned.
4. Chain, don't sprinkle: stun → `Existential Dread` the same target to
   bounce it; stun → kill it at `Leona - Zealot`'s battlefield; stun →
   `Solari Shrine` draws off the corpse.
5. `Monch` and `Eclipse Herald` are the tempo turns — both effectively
   cost less than their stats because the board is always stunned.
6. Win by attrition: their attacks keep failing, your holds keep
   drawing, and eventually `Vex - Mocking` plus the big bodies walk over
   an exhausted board.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- one cheap stun body (`Shadow`, `Solari Shieldbearer`);
- one stun spell (`Rune Prison`, `Existential Dread`, `Thwonk!`);
- one holder (`Trevor Snoozebottom`, `Nami - Headstrong`).

Avoid keeping `Eclipse Herald` or `Leona - Zealot` in openers without
early defense; the gloom needs a board to settle over.

## Watch Outs

- Stun prevents **combat damage only** — a stunned unit still conquers,
  holds, and uses abilities. Stun stops the sword, not the flag: to deny
  a score you must still out-body the battlefield.
- Stuns are "this turn": nothing stays stunned across turns, so `Leona -
  Zealot` and `Existential Dread`'s bounce need their setup stun **the
  same turn**.
- `Vex - Apathetic` only watches **her** battlefield, and the can't-move
  clause expires at end of turn.
- The Legend exhausts for its draw — one card per turn no matter how
  many battlefields you hold.
- `Shadow`'s stun only hits units **attacking here** — it defends its
  own ground, it doesn't project across the map.
- `Trevor`'s Sprite is [Temporary] and dies before scoring next turn —
  it's a blocker, not a scorer; keep a real unit at the point.

## Upgrade Paths

- `Facebreaker` (Order) is illegal here — stun packages must stay
  Calm/Chaos; `Skyward Strike` (move + stun at Level 6) is the in-domain
  addition if the deck adds XP sources.
- `Zhonya's Hourglass` protects a key wall from the one big attack that
  gets through.
- `Iascylla` (from the Ahri shell) drags victims into Leona's execution
  zone in a slower build.
- `Mageseeker Warden` closes the map entirely once you're ahead —
  opponents can only deploy to base while it holds a battlefield.
