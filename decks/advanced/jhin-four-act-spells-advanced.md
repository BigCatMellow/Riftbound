## Why This Is An Advanced Deck

`Jhin - Virtuoso` counts spells where you **spent** 4+ Energy. The deck
is built so a single big cast triggers a stack of payoffs at once:

- the Legend banks the spell (act 1 through 4 — the fourth spell
  refunds 4 runes and a card);
- `Prepared Neophyte` becomes a 5-Might attacker for the turn;
- `Revna the Lorekeeper` readies, and she has [Ganking] to spend that
  ready anywhere;
- `Jhin - Meticulous Killer` in hand now costs just a Mind Rune;
- `Lux - Illuminated` grows +3 if the spell's printed cost was 5+;
- at `Forgotten Library`, the same cast also Predicts.

The skill is sequencing: *what you actually spend* is what counts, so
cost reductions — normally pure upside — can silently turn off the
entire deck. That trap is why `Eager Apprentice` (reduces your spell
costs by 1) is deliberately absent.

  (printed directly after the Legend, UNL-181); 3 copies sit exactly at

- Banned/errata check: no card used here is marked Banned in the local
  card database.
- Champion tag: `Jhin - Meticulous Killer` is inferred to carry the
  `Jhin` champion tag from card name and the Legend's own name, per this
  project's convention. The source export has no explicit tag column —
  confirm the printed tag before tournament play.

## How To Play

1. Spend turns 1-2 on bodies and cheap interaction (`Ravenbloom
   Student`, `Hextech Ray`, `Prepared Neophyte`) — the deck's engine
   starts when your rune base can pay 4+ Energy comfortably.
2. From then on, aim for one "act" (a 4+ Energy spell, actually paying
   4+) per turn. Banish it to the Legend every time; the fourth act's
   4-rune refund usually funds a second spell that same turn.
3. Sequence payoffs before the act when they're units you're deploying
   (`Prepared Neophyte` attacks *after* the spell; `Jhin - Meticulous
   Killer` comes down for one rune *after* the spend).
4. `Fate Weaver` and `Progress Day` chain acts together; `Curtain Call`
   with two or three Repeats is the classic closer — each Repeat picks a
   new mode and each payment past the base cast is more Energy spent.
5. Hold one battlefield with bodies and let `Forgotten Library` or `The
   Academy` do quiet work — this deck wins long games, not races.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- one or two cheap bodies (`Ravenbloom Student`, `Prepared Neophyte`);
- one piece of cheap interaction (`Hextech Ray`, `Premonition`);
- one act you can reasonably cast by turn 3-4 (`Curtain Call`,
  `Disintegrate`, `Consult the Past`).

Avoid keeping double 6-drops; the deck needs to live to its act turns.

## Watch Outs

- **"Spent" means what you actually paid.** A `Consult the Past` played
  from hidden costs 0 — no Legend trigger, no Neophyte, no Revna. The
  same applies to any future cost reduction: `Eager Apprentice` is
  *intentionally excluded* because reducing a 4-cost spell to 3 turns
  off the whole deck. Check every discount before adding one.
- The Legend's banish is optional but binary: a banished spell is gone
  until the fourth act returns it. Don't banish `Progress Day` early if
  you expect to need it back — the refund only comes at exactly four.
- `Blind Fury` plays the stolen card ignoring its cost, so it does NOT
  count as spending that card's Energy for any trigger.
- `Rocket Barrage`'s Repeat cost (4 Energy + Mind Rune) is an
  *additional cost on the same spell*, not a new spell — count triggers
  from the play, not per repeat.
- `Lux - Illuminated` reads printed cost ("costs 5 or more"), not spend:
  `Curtain Call` (4) grows the Legend's clock but not her.

## Upgrade Paths

- `Energy Conduit` or `Hextech Anomaly` (Mind gear that adds Energy at
  reaction speed) smooths the jump to back-to-back acts.
- `Unchecked Power` (7: deal 12 to ALL units at battlefields) is a
  sideboard-style haymaker against go-wide decks — it hits your own
  board too, so it didn't make the main list.
- `Time Warp` (10: take an extra turn) is the ceiling-raiser if games
  regularly reach double-digit Energy; an extra turn with four acts
  banked usually ends the game.
