## Why This Is A Synergy Deck

`Rengar - Pridestalker`'s line looks small until you count how many
units this deck plays per turn, and *when*:

- `Rengar - Pouncing` is a [Reaction] **unit** playable into a
  battlefield you're attacking — a 3/3 Assault 2 body plus a Legend
  pump, in the middle of the fight;
- `Inferna`, `Lord Broadmane`, `Grim Apothecary`, `Nidalee - Cat
  Form`, and `Kha'Zix - Mutating Horror` all have [Ambush] — each is
  a reinforcement *and* a +1 wherever you need it;
- `Here to Help` (hidden) deploys a hand unit at -3 Energy mid-
  showdown — a discounted body plus the pump;
- `Thrill of the Hunt` (signature) banishes a friendly unit and
  replays it **to any battlefield**, free — a repositioning trick, a
  re-buy of any on-play effect, and yet another Legend pump;
- `Carnivorous Snapvine`'s on-play fight can be sequenced *after* the
  Legend's pump lands on it — the fight resolves with the +1 counted;
- `Rengar - Trophy Hunter` (chosen) is playable directly into enemy
  battlefields — the trophy walks into the den mid-turn.

## Legality Notes

- Domain identity: Rengar - Pridestalker is Fury/Body. Every main deck
  card is single-domain Fury, single-domain Body, or the Fury/Body
  Signature card `Thrill of the Hunt`.
- Copy-limit check: `Rengar - Trophy Hunter` has 1 chosen-champion copy
  plus 2 main deck copies (3 total, at the limit). `Rengar - Pouncing`
  and `Rengar - Unseen` are different card names. No other card exceeds
  3 copies.
- Signature check: `Thrill of the Hunt` (UNL-184) is Rengar's own
  signature (printed directly after the Legend, UNL-183); 3 copies sit
  exactly at the 3-signature limit and match the Legend's tag.
- Banned/errata check: no card used here is marked Banned in the local
  card database.
- Champion tag: the three Rengar units are inferred to carry the
  `Rengar` champion tag from card name and the Legend's own name, per
  this project's convention. The source export has no explicit tag
  column — confirm the printed tag before tournament play.

## How To Play

1. Hold units, not just spells: this deck's "combat tricks" are mostly
   creatures. Attack with less than you have and let [Ambush] and
   `Rengar - Pouncing` close the trap mid-showdown.
2. Aim the Legend's pump deliberately every time — it can target *any*
   unit, including the one you just played (a Pouncing that arrives as
   a 4/3 Assault 2) or a hunter already in the fight.
3. `Thrill of the Hunt` is three cards in one: dodge removal (banish),
   re-trigger an on-play (`Snapvine` fights again, `Broadmane` anthems
   again), and relocate the threat — plus the pump.
4. `Confront` turns a development turn into an attack turn: three
   units, three pumps, all entering ready.
5. `Kha'Zix - Mutating Horror` hunts loners — use `Hextech Ray` or a
   trade to strip a battlefield down to one defender first.
6. Sequence `Carnivorous Snapvine` pumps: play it, land the Legend's
   +1 on it, *then* let its fight resolve at 7.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- one cheap ambusher (`Inferna`, `Rengar - Pouncing`);
- one trick (`Cleave`, `Punch First`);
- `Kinkou Initiate` or `Nidalee` for the early board.

Avoid keeping slow 5-drops without early bodies — the pride hunts in
packs, from the start.

## Watch Outs

- The Legend's pump triggers on **playing** a unit — moved, readied, or
  cascaded units don't count; `Thrill of the Hunt`'s replay **does**
  (it's a play).
- The +1 lasts this turn — it's combat math, not growth. This deck has
  no permanent buffs at all; it wins fights, not stat wars.
- [Ambush] requires a battlefield **where you have units** — a fully
  lost battlefield can't be ambushed; keep one body everywhere that
  matters (`Rengar - Trophy Hunter` and `Dauntless Vanguard` are the
  exceptions that break this rule).
- `Here to Help` deploys to a battlefield **you control** — it
  reinforces, it doesn't invade.
- `Grim Apothecary`'s bounce is optional but tempting — re-buying an
  ambusher costs you its board presence now; rescue wounded hunters,
  not healthy ones.
- `Confront` only readies units played **after** it resolves this turn
  — cast it before the wave, not after.

## Upgrade Paths

- `Stormbringer` and `Relentless Pursuit` are Volibear's and Lucian's
  signatures — illegal here despite the domain match.
- `Perched Grimwyrm` rewards a more conquest-focused build.
- `Red Brambleback` doubles conquer effects if the deck drifts toward
  the Rek'Sai shell's plan.
- `Arena Kingpin` (exhaust: +3 this turn) adds a repeatable pump that
  doesn't need a unit played.
