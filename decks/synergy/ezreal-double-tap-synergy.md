## Why This Is A Synergy Deck

"Choose an enemy twice per turn" sounds like a cost until you notice
how much of it is free:

- `Orb of Regret` (1-cost gear): exhaust — give a unit -1 Might. One
  tap per turn, forever, no cards spent;
- `The List` (1-cost gear): exhaust — -2 Might to the named tag. The
  second free tap. Both gear out = the Legend draws **every turn** on
  autopilot;
- `Ezreal - Dashing` (chosen): when he attacks or defends, he deals his
  Might to an enemy unit here as an ability — combat itself taps;
- `Icevale Archer` and `Sinister Poro`: 2-drops whose attacks tap for
  1 Energy;
- `Arcane Shift` (signature): banish and free-replay your own unit
  (re-buying `Windsinger`'s or `Ezreal - Prodigy`'s on-play) *and*
  deal 3 to an enemy — a tap, a trick, and a value engine in one card;
- the entire interaction suite (`Stupefy`, `Smoke Screen`, `Frigid
  Touch`, `Eclipse`, `Gust`, `Rebuke`, `Isolate`, `Temptation`) taps as
  a side effect of doing its job.

The deck plays like a normal tempo-control list that happens to draw
one extra card nearly every turn.

## Legality Notes

- Domain identity: Ezreal - Prodigal Explorer is Mind/Chaos. Every main
  deck card is single-domain Mind, single-domain Chaos, or the
  Mind/Chaos Signature card `Arcane Shift`.
- Copy-limit check: `Ezreal - Dashing` has 1 chosen-champion copy plus
  2 main deck copies (3 total, at the limit). `Ezreal - Prodigy` is a
  different card name. No other card exceeds 3 copies.
- Signature check: `Arcane Shift` (SFD-200) is Ezreal's own signature
  (printed directly after the Legend, SFD-199); 3 copies sit exactly at
  the 3-signature limit and match the Legend's tag.
- Banned/errata check: no card used here is marked Banned in the local
  card database.
- Champion tag: `Ezreal - Dashing` and `Ezreal - Prodigy` are inferred
  to carry the `Ezreal` champion tag from card name and the Legend's
  own name, per this project's convention. The source export has no
  explicit tag column — confirm the printed tag before tournament play.

## How To Play

1. Turn 1-2: land `Orb of Regret` and `The List` (name the opponent's
   main champion tag). With both out, exhaust each on their turn or
   yours — the Legend now draws every single turn for free.
2. Play the taps as interaction first: shrink their attacker, bounce
   their blocker — the choose-count is bookkeeping on top of plays you
   wanted anyway.
3. `Ezreal - Dashing` fights like a mage: his combat presence is an
   ability (it taps and dodges "combat damage" effects). Mind Rune
   retreats him when the fight sours.
4. `Arcane Shift` best-case: banish `Windsinger` (bounce again on
   replay), deal 3 to their unit — one card produced two taps, two
   tempo swings, and a body reset.
5. The Legend is a **reaction**: you can tap twice during the
   opponent's combat and draw mid-fight.
6. Win slow: this is a tempo-attrition deck — the extra card per turn
   is the wincon, compounding until their threats run out before your
   answers.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- `Orb of Regret` or `The List` (the free-tap engines);
- one cheap tap body (`Icevale Archer`, `Blastcone Fae`, `Sinister
  Poro`);
- one interaction spell.

Avoid keeping hands with no 1-2 drops — the engine wants both gear on
the table by turn 2.

## Watch Outs

- The Legend counts choosing **enemy units and/or gear** with spells or
  abilities — attacking, blocking, and untargeted effects don't count,
  and choosing your *own* units (`Arcane Shift`'s banish half) doesn't
  either; only its deal-3 half taps.
- Two taps must land **this turn** — the count resets; one tap a turn
  forever draws nothing.
- `The List` names one tag at play time — against a tribe-less deck
  name the chosen champion's tag; a bad name makes it a dead gear.
- `Ezreal - Dashing` deals **no combat damage** — alone, he cannot
  conquer through a defender the ability doesn't kill.
- Min-1 floors on the -Might taps mean they interact, they don't
  execute — pair them with combat or bounce to finish.
- `Temptation` drags enemies **toward their own units** — it sets up
  your next fight, it can't strand them alone.

## Upgrade Paths

- `Spirit Wheel` is a *friendly*-choose engine (that's the Irelia
  shell); it does nothing here — a reminder the two "choose" decks
  read opposite halves of the rule.
- `Moonfall` and `Guerilla Warfare` are Diana's and Teemo's signatures
  and illegal despite the domain match.
- `Jae Medarda` doesn't trigger the Legend but rewards a hybrid
  friendly-targeting build if the pair's third deck ever merges shells.
- `Thousand-Tailed Watcher` (-3 to ALL enemies) is one giant tap and a
  finisher body for the long game.
