## Why This Is A Synergy Deck

`Irelia - Blade Dancer` reads: when you **choose** a friendly unit, you
may exhaust her and pay Any Rune to ready it. Choosing is the cheapest
thing a deck can do — every pump spell already does it — so the deck
stacks payoffs on the same trigger:

- `Irelia - Fervent`: +1 Might when chosen, +1 Might *again* when
  readied — the Legend's ready is itself another trigger;
- `Jae Medarda`: draw 1 when chosen with a spell;
- `Spirit Wheel`: pay 1 and exhaust when you choose a friendly unit —
  draw 1;
- `The Dreaming Tree` (battlefield): first friendly-unit spell choice
  each turn draws;
- `Irelia - Graceful`: spells choosing her cost 1 Energy or Any Rune
  less — the discount pays the Legend's ready cost;
- `Simian Ancestor`: readies whenever buffed — `Stand United` chooses,
  buffs, and untaps him all at once.

The ready effects aren't just value — they're extra combats. A pumped,
untapped `Irelia - Fervent` attacks twice a turn, and `Last Stand`
doubles whatever the triggers built.

  Signature card `Defiant Dance`.

  copies.
- Signature check: `Defiant Dance` (SFD-196) is Irelia's own signature
  (printed directly after the Legend, SFD-195); 3 copies sit exactly at
  the 3-signature limit and match the Legend's tag.
- Banned/errata check: no card used here is marked Banned in the local
  card database.
- Champion tag: `Irelia - Fervent` and `Irelia - Graceful` are inferred
  to carry the `Irelia` champion tag from card name and the Legend's own
  name, per this project's convention. The source export has no explicit
  tag column — confirm the printed tag before tournament play.

## How To Play

1. Deploy a target-worthy body early (`Wielder of Water`, `Yuumi`,
   `Ribbon Dancer`) and start choosing it — even a bare `En Garde` with
   the Legend up is +2 Might and an untap for two total Energy.
2. Get `Jae Medarda`, `Spirit Wheel`, or `The Dreaming Tree` online
   before spending your tricks in bulk — the same spells you'd cast
   anyway start drawing cards.
3. `Irelia - Fervent` is the payoff stack: `Discipline` on her with the
   Legend ready is +2 (spell) +1 (chosen) +1 (readied), a draw, and an
   untapped attacker.
4. Conquer with the readied unit, then pay 1 to re-ready the Legend
   (her second line) — at `Hall of Legends` this loop runs twice a turn.
5. `Last Stand` ends games: double a Fervent that's already at 8-9
   Might. The [Temporary] death is next turn's problem; the game often
   isn't that long.
6. `Defiant Dance` is your removal — the -2 half shrinks a blocker while
   the +2 half triggers everything.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- one cheap body worth targeting (`Wielder of Water`, `Yuumi`,
  `Ribbon Dancer`);
- two cheap tricks (`En Garde`, `Defiant Dance`, `Discipline`);
- `Spirit Wheel` if the curve allows.

Avoid keeping `Last Stand` or `Simian Ancestor` in openers with no
cheap units — the engine needs a blade before it can dance.

## Watch Outs

- The Legend's ready costs her exhaust plus Any Rune — once per turn
  unless a conquer (plus 1 Energy) rewinds her. Budget the rune.
- "Choose" means targeting: auras and untargeted effects (`Stand
  United`'s global buff clause, battlefield triggers) don't choose and
  don't trigger the Legend.
- `Irelia - Fervent`'s bonuses are "this turn" — she resets each turn;
  the permanent growth comes from `Stand United` buffs, not her
  triggers.
- `Last Stand`'s [Temporary] is real: the doubled unit dies at the start
  of your next Beginning Phase, before scoring. Close with it, don't
  develop with it.
- `Spirit Wheel`'s draw costs 1 Energy and its exhaust — one per turn,
  and it competes with the Legend for loose runes.
- Readying a unit does not heal it or clear stuns — an untapped stunned
  unit still deals no combat damage this turn.

## Upgrade Paths

- `Shurelya's Requiem` is Ornn's signature and illegal here; the
  in-domain mass-ready is `On the Hunt` — which is Sivir's, also
  illegal. Mass readies must come from repeated Legend triggers; build
  around that ceiling.
- `Irelia - Graceful` plus more expensive targeting spells (`Block`,
  `Tricksy Tentacles`) deepen the discount engine.
- `Zhonya's Hourglass` protects the built-up blade from the removal
  that inevitably comes for it.
- A defensive version swaps `Last Stand` for `Emperor's Divide` (mass
  self-recall) and leans on `Yuumi` walls.
