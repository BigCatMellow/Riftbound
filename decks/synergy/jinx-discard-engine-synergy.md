## Why This Is A Synergy Deck

Almost every non-removal card in this list cares about discarding, being
discarded, or refilling the hand afterward. The deck is built around one
resource loop: discard outlets feed `Jinx - Rebel`'s ready-and-grow trigger,
`Flame Chompers` turns a discard into a free replay, and `Super Mega Death
Rocket!` comes back from the trash instead of staying dead. The payoff is
overlapping triggers on a single card play, not raw stats.

## Legality Notes

- Domain identity: Jinx - Loose Cannon is Fury/Chaos. Every main deck card is
  single-domain Fury, single-domain Chaos, or the dual Fury/Chaos card
  `Super Mega Death Rocket!`, so all cards fit the identity.
- Copy-limit check: `Jinx - Rebel` has 1 chosen-champion copy plus 2 main deck
  copies (3 total). No other card exceeds 3 copies.
- Signature check: the only signature card is `Super Mega Death Rocket!`, at 3
  copies total, which is the deck's full signature allowance.
- Banned/errata check: `Scrapheap` (OGN-182) is marked **Banned** in the local
  card database and is intentionally excluded from this list. No other card
  used here is banned.
- Champion tag: `Jinx - Rebel` and `Jinx - Demolitionist` are inferred to
  carry the `Jinx` champion tag from card name and the Legend's own name.
  The source export has no explicit tag column — confirm the printed tag on
  the physical/digital card before tournament play.

## How To Play

1. Play cheap discard outlets (`Chemtech Enforcer`, `Traveling Merchant`,
   `Evershade Stalker`) early to start triggering `Jinx - Rebel` before she is
   even out.
2. Once `Jinx - Rebel` is on board, sequence any discard effect after combat
   so she readies and gets +1 Might to threaten again immediately.
3. Use `Get Excited!` and `Super Mega Death Rocket!` as your removal; both
   double as discard triggers when you have excess cards.
4. Let `Flame Chompers` sit in your discard outlets on purpose — a Fury Rune
   later brings it back as a free body.
5. Hold `Undercover Agent` and `Scrapyard Champion` as refuel points when your
   hand runs dry from aggressive discarding.
6. Recur `Super Mega Death Rocket!` by conquering and discarding a spare card,
   turning one removal spell into a repeatable one.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- one cheap discard outlet: `Chemtech Enforcer`, `Traveling Merchant`, or
  `Evershade Stalker`;
- one piece of removal: `Get Excited!` or `Super Mega Death Rocket!`;
- `Jinx - Rebel` or `Jinx - Demolitionist` if you already have an outlet to
  support them.

Avoid keeping `Undercover Agent` or `Scrapyard Champion` alone without any
early discard outlet — they want the loop already running.

## Watch Outs

- The deck can empty its hand quickly. Track how many cards you have left
  before triggering `Corrupt Enforcer` or `Traveling Merchant` repeatedly.
- `Get Excited!`'s damage equals the discarded card's Energy cost — discard a
  high-cost dead card for a big hit, not your best remaining play.
- `Bewitching Spirit` can target either player; only target yourself when you
  need the Jinx trigger and can afford the card loss.
- Opponents with heavy removal can pick off `Jinx - Rebel` before she grows
  large. Keep a second discard-payoff threat ready as backup.
- Don't play `Get Excited!` (or any other "discard 1, then use the discarded
  card" effect) when it would be the last card in your hand. The card
  discards itself out of your hand as part of being played, so if nothing
  else is left, "discard 1" resolves to zero cards and the follow-up damage
  has no card to reference — a complete whiff for 2 Energy. Confirmed in
  `simulations/SIM-0002-jinx-vs-masteryi-rules-smoke.md` (Turns 4 and 5).

## Upgrade Paths

- Add more Chaos card selection (`Stacked Deck`, `Abandon`) if the deck floods
  or screws on lands too often.
- Swap `Detonate` for a second `Lunar Boon` in metas without much gear to
  target.
- For a lower-complexity version, cut `Corrupt Enforcer` and `Traveling
  Merchant` (both need repositioning to trigger) for straightforward
  discard-on-play units.
