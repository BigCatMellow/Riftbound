## Why This Is A Synergy Deck

The buff economy has three loops feeding each other:

- **Mint**: `Lee Sin - Blind Monk` (1 Energy: buff, every turn),
  `Pit Rookie`, `Cithria of Cloudfield`, `Sea Monkey`, `Stand United`,
  `Arena Bar`, `Fae Dragon` (buffs four at once), and `Navori Fighting
  Pit` on hold;
- **Hold**: `Lee Sin - Ascetic` stacks unlimited buffs and readies
  himself to mint more; `Wizened Elder` gets +1 extra while buffed;
  `Lee Sin - Centered` gives every *other* buffed unit at his
  battlefield +2 — the anthem reads the currency without spending it;
- **Spend**: `Wallop` (spend a buff → the spell is free), `Wildclaw
  Shaman` (spend → re-buff and ready himself), `Kraken Hunter` (spend
  buffs → cost reduction), `Overt Operation` (spend any number → ready
  those units, then re-buff everyone), `Fae Dragon` (every spent buff
  anywhere → a Gold token), and `Monastery of Hirana` (conquer → spend
  a buff → draw).

Because `Simian Ancestor` readies whenever buffed and `Mistfall`
converts any buff into an untap, the mint loop is also an attack-step
loop — the treasury fights.

## How To Play

1. Mint from turn 1: the Legend's 1-Energy buff should almost never be
   skipped. Early coins go on `Wizened Elder` (worth double) or
   `Cithria` (compounds on her own).
2. Get `Spirit's Refuge` down before the board is worth killing —
   [Deflect] on every buffed unit makes targeted removal expensive
   everywhere at once.
3. `Lee Sin - Ascetic` banks on quiet turns: exhaust him to self-buff
   whenever he has nothing to fight, and let `Mistfall` or `Simian`
   untap him when he does.
4. Spend deliberately: `Wallop` for a surprise second attack, `Kraken
   Hunter` when the treasury is rich, `Overt Operation` as the alpha
   strike that re-mints afterward.
5. With `Fae Dragon` out, every spend is also a Gold token — the
   `Overt Operation` turn can mint four Golds while readying the army.
6. `Dragon's Rage` solves what combat can't: pull their wall into
   their own attacker and let them trade with themselves.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- two cheap bodies (`Cithria`, `Pit Rookie`, `Sea Monkey`);
- `Wizened Elder` or `Spirit's Refuge` as the early payoff;
- `Meditation` for fuel.

Avoid keeping `Fae Dragon` or `Overt Operation` in openers with no
cheap units — the economy needs citizens before it needs a treasury.

## Watch Outs

- **One buff per unit**: "buff" gives a +1 Might buff only *if the unit
  doesn't already have one* — the treasury spreads wide, it doesn't
  stack. The single exception is `Lee Sin - Ascetic`'s printed "any
  number of buffs" — that's why he's the bank.
- Spending a buff removes the +1: `Overt Operation`'s readies shrink
  the army until its second half re-buffs. Sequence attacks before the
  spend resolves your combat math wrongly.
- `Lee Sin - Centered` boosts *other* buffed units *at his battlefield*
  — he doesn't pump himself, and coins in base earn nothing from him.
- `Wallop` readies but doesn't protect — a readied unit can still be
  attacked into.
- `Dragon's Rage` needs **two** enemy units, one at the destination —
  it can't snipe a lone threat.
- `Arena Bar` only buffs **exhausted** units — the mint that pays
  veterans, not fresh deploys.

## Upgrade Paths

- `Sett - Brawler` and the Sett shell's spend-to-save trick live in
  Body/Order — for this pair, `Highlander` is Master Yi's signature and
  illegal; protection upgrades come from more `Spirit's Refuge` effects
  or `Zhonya's Hourglass`.
- `Wildclaw Shaman` ×3 and `Mistfall` ×3 tighten the untap loop for a
  more aggressive build.
- `Whiteflame Protector` (+8 this turn on play) is the burst finisher
  if games end faster than the compound interest matures.
- `Lonely Poro` plus `Mask of Foresight` support a lone-attacker
  variant that banks everything on one insured body.
