## Why This Is An Overpowered Deck

Most cards here do normal, fair things on their own — small units that gain
XP when they conquer or hold. The deck is pushed because the payoffs are
board-wide and permanent instead of one-shot: at 6+ XP, the Legend gives
**every** friendly unit +1 Might and the entire deck's damage output steps up
for the rest of the game; at 11+ XP, every friendly unit enters the board
ready, which removes the normal one-attack-per-unit-per-turn tempo tax
entirely. `Master Yi - Unstoppable` compounds this: its own cost keeps
dropping as your XP climbs, and at 16 XP it can no longer be chosen by enemy
spells or abilities at all. A deck built around a shared counter that scales
the whole board and eventually makes your best card immune to removal is the
kind of build worth flagging before a casual game, not springing on someone
mid-match.

## Legality Notes

- Domain identity: Master Yi - Wuju Master is Calm/Body. Every main deck card
  is single-domain Calm, single-domain Body, or the dual `Alpha Strike`
  (Calm, Body), so all cards fit the identity.
- Copy-limit check: `Master Yi - Unstoppable` has 1 chosen-champion copy plus
  2 main deck copies (3 total). No other card exceeds 3 copies.
- Signature check: no signature cards are included.
- Banned/errata check: no card used here is marked banned in the local card
  database.
- Champion tag: `Master Yi - Unstoppable` and `Master Yi - Tempered` are
  inferred to carry the `Master Yi` champion tag from card name and the
  Legend's own name. The source export has no explicit tag column — confirm
  the printed tag before tournament play.

## How To Play

1. Prioritize conquering or holding with any [Hunt] unit every turn possible
   — the exact battlefield matters less than triggering XP consistently.
2. Track your XP total openly. At 6, every friendly unit is permanently +1
   Might; replan combat math the turn this flips.
3. At 11 XP, every friendly unit enters ready. From this point you can play
   and immediately attack with everything that turn — sequence your biggest
   push for right after crossing this threshold.
4. Play `Master Yi - Unstoppable` as soon as its reduced cost fits your
   Energy, even before it's fully online — the body alone is a 12/12, and it
   only gets safer to keep on board as XP climbs further.
5. Use `Alpha Strike` off a big Mighty unit for a one-sided board wipe once
   the Level 6 buff has made your units bigger than the opponent's.
6. Bank `Show of Strength` for after the Level 6 threshold, when most of your
   board qualifies as Mighty and the card draws several cards at once.

## Mulligan

Keep:

- two cheap [Hunt] units to start the XP counter: `Gemhand Hunter`, `Wuju
  Apprentice`, or `Mosstomper`;
- one combat trick: `Grim Resolve`, `Stare Down`, or `Combat Experience`;
- `Master Yi - Tempered` if you also have an early Hunt unit to pair with it.

Avoid keeping `Master Yi - Unstoppable` or `Targonian Visionary` in an opening
hand with no early XP sources — both want XP already banked to be worth their
slot.

## Watch Outs

- This is the deck's own strongest tool against an unprepared opponent: a
  board-wide permanent Might buff plus a stage where every unit enters ready
  is genuinely oppressive in a casual pod. Agree on power level with the
  table before bringing this deck to a non-competitive game.
- Removal that hits before Level 6 is much more effective against this deck
  than removal that arrives after — opponents who interact early can prevent
  the snowball outright.
- `Master Yi - Unstoppable`'s anti-targeting clause only starts at 16 XP,
  which is a high bar; don't assume it's safe from removal before then.
- The deck has almost no hard removal of its own (`Alpha Strike` needs a big
  unit first). It out-values rather than out-removes, so a fast aggressive
  opponent can race it before XP accumulates.
- `Reckoner's Arena`'s hold trigger against [Hunt]-style units is a genuine
  open rules question (does it double the XP gain on a hold, or does it
  have nothing extra to activate since Hunt's hold branch already covers
  the same event?) — confirmed unresolved in
  `simulations/SIM-0008-masteryi-vs-lucian-rules-smoke.md`. Play it as a
  possible bonus, not a guaranteed XP doubler, until that's settled.

## Upgrade Paths

- Add `Poppy - Paragon` (Body) as a second late-game XP-into-stats payoff if
  games regularly run long.
- Add more XP-gain-on-play effects if games end before Level 11 is reached
  consistently.
- For a lower-power, more casual-friendly version, cut `Master Yi -
  Unstoppable` for a normal-cost finisher and treat the Level 6 board buff as
  the deck's ceiling instead of also chasing Level 11 and Level 16.
